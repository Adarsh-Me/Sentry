## 1. Executive Summary

India's space sector is scaling fast — ISRO's cadence is up, and private smallsat operators are launching under the IN-SPACe framework. Collision-risk (conjunction) screening for these assets still depends heavily on foreign tracking infrastructure, principally the U.S. Space Force's 18th Space Defense Squadron / USSPACECOM, which publishes conjunction data via Space-Track.org. There is no lightweight, transparent, India-built tool that lets an operator, researcher, or regulator independently screen for close-approach risk using open orbital data.

**Project KAVACH** ("kavach" = shield/armor) is a conjunction-assessment screening tool: it ingests public two-line-element (TLE) orbital data, propagates satellite positions forward in time using validated orbital mechanics (SGP4), screens all object pairs for close approaches below a configurable threshold, computes an explainable risk score (distance **and** closing velocity), and renders the result as a live, rotatable 3D scene of Earth with orbits — flagging conjunctions in a way a judge understands in under 10 seconds without any explanation.

This is explicitly a **screening/triage layer**, not a replacement for operational-grade conjunction assessment. That distinction is a stated product principle, not a disclaimer bolted on at the end.

**Project codename:** KAVACH-LEO (placeholder — team can rename; used consistently across all 8 planning docs for traceability. Elevator pitch line uses this name.)

## 2. Why This Problem Statement (Strategic Rationale)

This section exists because the source material ("The Intimidation Factor") makes an unusually explicit case for *why* this idea over the other 9 in the batch, and that reasoning should be preserved as a first-class product decision, not lost:

| Factor | Reasoning |
|---|---|
| **Uniqueness / "Intimidation Factor"** | Orbital mechanics *sounds* like rocket science, so most teams avoid it. It is actually a well-documented, library-solved problem (SGP4 via `skyfield`/`sgp4`). The team gets credit for apparent difficulty at a fraction of the real difficulty. |
| **Not saturated** | Unlike crop-disease detection, generic chatbots, or generic credit scoring, almost no national-hackathon team attempts orbital mechanics. Direct competitors (#5 grid SCADA IDS, #10 sovereign vector-DB) are conceptually strong but have become common "anomaly dashboard" framings judges have seen many times. |
| **Demo strength** | This is the single most visually self-evident idea in the batch: two 3D orbits converging, a computed miss-distance/risk score, and a highlighted "moment of closest approach" reads as impressive to a **non-technical judge** with zero narration needed. |
| **Feasibility** | This is fundamentally a data-pipeline + scoring-model problem wearing an aerospace costume. No team member needs to derive orbital mechanics — `skyfield` handles SGP4 propagation end-to-end. |
| **Theme fit** | Answers Track 04 ("satellite data analysis... aerospace tech") and Track 06 ("sovereign technology... foreign tech par depend na karein") simultaneously, without any stretch-framing. |

**The single biggest execution risk this PRD is built around:** the backend logic is comparatively easy; **the win/loss condition is the frontend visualization quality.** Every downstream document (TechSpec, Design, AppFlow) treats the 3D scene as a first-class deliverable, not a "nice to have."

## 4. Problem Statement

### 4.1 Core Problem
Indian satellite operators — government and private — lack an independent, domestically-built tool to assess collision risk. Screening capability today is either:
- Entirely outsourced to foreign tracking data (a stated dependency risk under India's technology sovereignty policy direction), or
- Absent altogether for smaller/newer operators without budget for commercial-grade Space Situational Awareness (SSA).

### 4.2 Why Now
- LEO congestion is accelerating globally as mega-constellations (Starlink, OneWeb, and upcoming Indian constellations) populate common orbital shells.
- Every collision generates debris that becomes a future collision risk (Kessler syndrome) — early screening has compounding returns.
- India's policy direction explicitly favors reduced dependency on foreign/unverified technology in strategically sensitive sectors, of which SSA is one.

### 4.3 Who Feels This
- **Private Indian smallsat startups** under IN-SPACe with no in-house SSA capability.
- **Academic/research satellite programs** (university cubesats) with no tracking budget.
- **Regulatory bodies** (IN-SPACe, ISRO) wanting an independent, auditable cross-check alongside foreign-sourced conjunction data.

### 5.2 Round 2 Goals (harden after R1 clears)
1. Full 15–20 satellite curated dataset including named Indian satellites for narrative weight.
2. FastAPI backend with documented endpoints (stretch from v1.0, promoted here because Round 2 explicitly wants a working prototype + README, and an API layer strengthens the "infrastructure others could plug into" pitch).
3. Polished React + 3D frontend (see TechSpec/Design) replacing any quick-and-dirty Round-1 scaffolding.
4. Automated basic tests for the screening and scoring logic.
5. Mandatory README written to submission standard.

### 5.3 Product-Vision Goals (stated on roadmap slide, not built)
1. Scale from curated subset to full-catalog screening (10,000+ objects) via spatial pre-filtering (octree/box search).
2. Direct API integration with an Indian satellite operator's telemetry systems.
3. Precision maneuver-planning (delta-v) recommendations beyond a monitoring window.
4. Historical conjunction analytics / trend reporting for shells relevant to Indian assets.

### 5.4 Explicit Non-Goals (say this out loud in the deck — it is a credibility strength)
- **Not** a replacement for operational USSPACECOM/18th Space Defense Squadron-grade conjunction data messages.
- **Not** claiming sub-kilometer precision — TLE/SGP4 propagation has well-documented error that grows with time-since-epoch.
- **Not** computing actual delta-v burn/maneuver plans in this version — only a monitoring/action time window.
- **Not** full-catalog (10,000+ object) screening in this version — a curated, clearly-labeled subset.

## 8. Functional Requirements

### 8.1 Data Ingestion (Must-Have)
- FR-1.1: Fetch current TLE data from a public source (Celestrak primary).
- FR-1.2: Cache fetched TLE data locally (file + metadata: fetch timestamp, group, satellite count) to survive API/network failure during a live demo.
- FR-1.3: Support filtering by constellation/group (Starlink, OneWeb, active, stations) **plus** a fixed allow-list of named Indian satellites (e.g. Cartosat, RISAT, EOS, IRNSS series — exact NORAD IDs to be confirmed against Celestrak at build time) fetched individually and merged into the working set for narrative relevance, even if they are not the pair that trips the alert.
- FR-1.4: Display TLE epoch (data freshness) so the user understands potential propagation error.

### 8.2 Orbit Propagation (Must-Have)
- FR-2.1: Propagate each satellite's ECI position over a configurable window (default 24h) at a configurable step (default 5 min).
- FR-2.2: Use a standard, validated model — SGP4 via `skyfield`.
- FR-2.3: Handle propagation failures (decayed/invalid TLEs) gracefully without crashing the pipeline; surface a visible warning instead.
- FR-2.4 *(new in v2.0)*: When a coarse (5-min step) pass flags a pair below threshold, re-propagate that pair at a fine step (e.g. 10 seconds) in a ±15-minute window around the coarse minimum, to report a tighter, more defensible closest-approach estimate. This is a small addition with outsized "Reason" credibility — it shows iterative refinement, not a single blunt pass.

### 8.3 Conjunction Screening (Must-Have)
- FR-3.1: Compute pairwise distances across all selected satellites over the propagated window.
- FR-3.2: Flag any pair whose minimum distance falls below a configurable threshold (default 50 km), clearly labeled as a **"monitoring threshold,"** not a collision threshold.
- FR-3.3: Record time of closest approach for each flagged pair.
- FR-3.4: Support re-running screening interactively when the threshold or window is adjusted in the UI.

### 8.4 Risk Scoring (Must-Have — upgraded scope from v1.0)
- FR-4.1: Compute a normalized 0–100 risk score per flagged conjunction using **two factors**, both disclosed in the UI:
  - **Proximity factor** (weight 70%): how far below threshold the minimum distance is.
  - **Closing-speed factor** (weight 30%): relative velocity at closest approach, normalized against a reference LEO relative-velocity range — higher relative speed at a given distance means less human reaction time, which is a real and explainable urgency signal.
- FR-4.2: Bucket into Low / Medium / High for fast interpretation (thresholds: <35 Low, 35–69 Medium, ≥70 High — unchanged from v1.0 for consistency).
- FR-4.3: Display the scoring formula in-app — no black-box scoring, ever. This directly serves the "Reason" judging pillar and the "auditable, not black-box" sovereignty pitch.

### 8.5 Monitoring Window (Should-Have)
- FR-5.1: Suggest a monitoring/action time window (default ±30 min around closest approach) for flagged pairs, scaled slightly wider for HIGH risk.
- FR-5.2: Explicitly labeled a "monitoring window," never a precision maneuver/burn plan.

### 8.6 Visualization (Must-Have — this is the P0 differentiator, see `Design.md` and `TechSpec.md`)
- FR-6.1: Render an interactive 3D scene of Earth with all selected satellite orbits.
- FR-6.2: Visually distinguish flagged conjunctions (color, glow/pulse, thickness) from normal orbits.
- FR-6.3: Clicking an alert smoothly flies the camera to that conjunction and shows a synchronized info card (distance, risk score, time window).
- FR-6.4: Distinguish real Indian satellites in the scene with a distinct marker/label so the sovereignty narrative is visible, not just claimed in text.
- FR-6.5: Scene must run smoothly (no visible stutter) with the full curated dataset on a standard laptop/projector — a laggy "wow moment" is worse than no wow moment.

### 8.7 Dashboard / UI (Must-Have)
- FR-7.1: Summary table/panel of all flagged conjunctions (pair, min distance, risk score/level, time window), sorted by severity.
- FR-7.2: 3D visualization and alerts panel visible together, not on separate tabs — a judge should see both without clicking.
- FR-7.3: A visible, permanent disclaimer/caveat section describing accuracy limitations (SGP4/TLE error growth, monitoring vs collision threshold).
- FR-7.4 *(new)*: A small, persistent "Perceive → Reason → Execute" strip mapping each pipeline stage to what's currently happening on screen (loading = Perceive, scoring shown = Reason, alert + window = Execute) — makes the judging-rubric fit undeniable rather than something the presenter has to explain verbally.

### 8.8 API Layer (Should-Have for Round 2, not required for Round 1)
- FR-8.1: REST endpoint(s) accepting a satellite selection and screening parameters, returning conjunction/risk results as JSON.
- FR-8.2: Basic auto-generated docs (FastAPI's built-in OpenAPI/Swagger is sufficient) — strengthens the "infrastructure others could plug into" pitch for Round 2 and beyond.

## 9. Non-Functional Requirements

| Category | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Performance** | Full screening + propagation for up to 20 satellites over a 24h window completes in under 30 seconds on a standard laptop; 3D scene renders at an interactive frame rate (target ≥30fps) with the full dataset loaded. |

| **Data Freshness** | TLE data is re-fetchable on demand; fetch timestamp/epoch always visible. |
| **Compliance** | All code MIT-licensed per competition rules (see `Rules.md`); no credentials/API keys committed to the repo. |

## 10. Technical Architecture (Summary — full detail in `TechSpec.md`)

Two-service architecture: a Python **backend** (FastAPI + skyfield/sgp4 + numpy/pandas) doing TLE ingestion, propagation, screening, and scoring; and a **React frontend** (react-three-fiber/Three.js for the 3D scene + a conventional dashboard shell) consuming a small JSON API. This replaces the original Streamlit + Plotly single-process design from v1.0 — the change is deliberate and is the single highest-leverage decision in this PRD, made in direct response to the explicit mentor guidance that visualization quality decides the outcome. See `TechSpec.md §2` for the full justification and a documented fallback path if the team is short on frontend time.

## 11. Scope Definition

### 11.1 In Scope
- Curated set of 15–20 satellites: a dense same-constellation subset (Starlink or OneWeb) for a guaranteed genuine close approach, plus 2–5 named real Indian satellites for narrative relevance.
- 12–24 hour propagation window (configurable).
- Single distance-based screening threshold (configurable), enhanced with the velocity-based risk factor.
- Deterministic, formula-based risk scoring (not ML-based in this version — explainability is a stated strength, not a limitation, for a sovereignty/trust-focused judging panel).
- Interactive 3D visualization (react-three-fiber), not static.
- Local-only deployment for the demo; no cloud hosting required.

### 11.2 Explicitly Out of Scope
- Full NORAD catalog screening (10,000+ objects).
- Machine-learning-based risk prediction (stated roadmap item only).
- Real orbital maneuver/burn computation.
- Integration with classified/restricted defense tracking data.
- Native mobile app.

## 13. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Random satellite selection yields zero real conjunctions in the demo window | High | High (kills the "wow" moment) | Use same-constellation objects (Starlink/OneWeb); widen threshold to 50–100km, clearly labeled a monitoring threshold |
| TLE accuracy degrades with time-since-epoch | Medium | Medium | Re-fetch fresh TLEs immediately before any recorded demo; keep propagation window short (12–24h) |
| 3D scene becomes slow/laggy with too many objects/timesteps | Medium | Medium | Cap object count (15–20) and window length; profile early, not the night before |
| Frontend rewrite (Streamlit → React/3D) eats the timeline | Medium | High | Start the 3D scene in Week 1 as a spike, not last; keep a documented Streamlit+Plotly fallback path (see `TechSpec.md §2.3`) if the 3D build stalls |
| Live demo breaks (wifi/API down) during actual presentation | Low–Medium | High | Cache all TLE data locally in advance; always have a pre-recorded backup video |
| Judges question scientific accuracy/precision | High (expected) | Low if handled well | Disclose SGP4/TLE limitations proactively in the deck; frame explicitly as "screening/triage," not operational-grade SSA |
| Team misjudges the competition as a single overnight hackathon and burns effort in the wrong order | Medium (already happened once in v1.0) | Medium | This PRD and `Tracker.md` are explicitly organized by the real Round 1/2/3 dates |
