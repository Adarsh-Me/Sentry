# Design Specification
## Project KAVACH вҖ” Indigenous LEO Satellite Conjunction-Assessment Tool

**Companion to:** `PRD.md`, `TechSpec.md`, `AppFlow.md`
**Version:** 2.0

---

## 1. Design Principles

1. **"Mission control," not "SaaS dashboard."** This is a sovereignty pitch вҖ” it should feel like it belongs in an operations room, not like a generic analytics product. Think ISRO Mission Control Centre displays, not a startup landing page.
2. **The visual is the argument.** Per `PRD.md В§2`, the 3D scene carries more persuasive weight than any slide of text. Every design decision should ask "does this make the converging-orbits moment land harder, or does it dilute it?"
3. **Legible at a distance, in a hurry.** Judges see this on a shared screen or projector for a few minutes. No small text, no relying on subtle color differences alone, no cluttered chrome competing with the globe.
4. **Honesty is a design value, not just a copy value.** The disclaimer/caveat section is designed to be genuinely visible (not a grey footnote), because the project's whole credibility argument rests on "we tell you exactly what this is and isn't."

---

## 2. Visual Identity

### 2.1 Color Palette

| Role | Color | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Background (scene + app shell) | Deep space navy/near-black вҖ” `#05070D` to `#0B0F1A` | Sets the "mission control" tone; makes glowing elements pop |
| Earth sphere | Muted blue-teal gradient, low-poly or subtly textured вҖ” `#1C4E66` base | Stylized, not photorealistic вҖ” avoids needing external imagery/tokens (see `TechSpec.md В§2.2`) |
| Normal orbit lines | Soft cyan/white вҖ” `#8FD9FF` at low opacity (~40%) | Recedes visually so flagged orbits stand out |
| Flagged/alert orbit + marker | Alert red вҖ” `#FF3B3B`, with a soft outer glow | The one color that should dominate attention when present |
| Medium risk accent | Amber вҖ” `#FFB020` | Used in `RiskBadge`, not in the 3D scene (keep the scene's red reserved for the highest-signal moment) |
| Low risk accent | Green/cyan вҖ” `#3DDC97` | `RiskBadge` only |
| Indian-asset marker accent | A restrained saffron/white/green tricolor-inspired ring around the marker dot вҖ” used sparingly, only as a small accent ring, never as a loud banner | Ties the sovereignty narrative visually to specific satellites without looking gimmicky |
| UI chrome (panels, text) | Near-white `#E8ECF1` on the dark background; secondary text `#8B93A7` | High contrast for distance-legibility |

### 2.2 Typography
- **Primary UI font:** a clean geometric sans (e.g. "Inter" or "IBM Plex Sans") for panel text, labels, buttons.
- **Data/monospace font:** "IBM Plex Mono" or "JetBrains Mono" for numeric readouts (distance, scores, timestamps) вҖ” monospace numbers look "instrumented," reinforcing the mission-control feel, and align cleanly in tables.
- **Minimum body size:** 14px equivalent in the deployed app; risk scores and satellite names in the alert list no smaller than 16px вҖ” this is a distance-legibility requirement, not a preference.

### 2.3 Iconography
- Minimal, line-style icons only (satellite, alert triangle, clock for monitoring window). Avoid busy or skeuomorphic icon sets вҖ” they compete with the glow effects in the 3D scene.

## 4. 3D Scene Design (`OrbitScene`)

- **Earth:** a sphere (radius scaled consistently with the km-based orbit data) with a subtle latitude/longitude grid overlay at very low opacity вҖ” communicates "this is a real coordinate space," not decoration.
- **Orbit paths:** thin lines (see palette above) traced from each satellite's propagated position array. Line thickness ~1.5px normal, ~4px + glow for a flagged pair.
- **Satellite markers:** small glowing points at the satellite's *current* position along its path (or at the scrubbed time if a time-scrubber is added as a stretch feature); hover reveals a label (name + `is_indian_asset` tricolor ring if applicable).
- **Idle state:** slow, continuous auto-rotate around the Earth when nothing is selected вҖ” keeps the screen visually alive during any dead air in a live demo.
- **Alert highlight:** at the moment of closest approach for a flagged pair, render a short connecting line between the two satellites with a pulsing marker at its midpoint (2вҖ“3 second pulse loop, not distractingly fast).
- **Camera fly-to:** on alert selection, animate the camera position + look-at target over ~1.5s with an eased curve (not linear вҖ” a linear camera move reads as robotic) to frame both satellites and the connecting line comfortably in view, then hold.

---

## 5. Motion & Animation Guidelines

| Interaction | Duration | Easing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Camera fly-to on alert select | ~1.5s | ease-in-out | The core "winning moment" вҖ” deliberate, not instant, so it reads as a discovery rather than a jump-cut |
| Alert marker pulse | 2вҖ“3s loop | ease-in-out (breathing, not blinking) | Draws the eye without being obnoxious on a long demo loop |
| Panel slide-in (`ScoreBreakdown`) | ~300ms | ease-out | Standard UI responsiveness, secondary to the camera motion |
| Idle globe auto-rotate | continuous, slow (~1 full rotation per 60вҖ“90s) | linear | Ambient, not a focal animation |

**Rule of thumb:** the *only* animation that should ever feel emphasized is the alert fly-to. Everything else is quiet by comparison, so the one moment that matters doesn't compete with itself.

## 6. Component Specs

### `AlertCard`
- Satellite pair names (monospace).
- Min distance in km (large, prominent number).
- `RiskBadge` (color-coded pill, LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH вҖ” text label always included, never color alone, for accessibility).
- Time-to-closest-approach, relative to "now" if within the window (e.g. "in 6h 45m").
- Click target is the whole card, not just a small button вҖ” easy to hit live during a demo.

### `RiskBadge`
- Pill shape, colored per В§2.1, with the risk level as visible text (not just color) вҖ” satisfies color-blind accessibility and remains legible on a low-quality video recording.

### `ScoreBreakdown`
- Shows the formula from `TechSpec.md В§4.4` with the actual computed numbers substituted in, e.g.:
  `Proximity: 0.82 Г— 70% = 57.4  +  Closing speed: 0.34 Г— 30% = 10.2  вҶ’  Score: 67.6 (MEDIUM)`
- This concrete, plugged-in presentation is what makes "no black-box scoring" a demonstrated fact rather than a claim.

### `ControlPanel`
- Threshold slider with the current value always shown numerically next to the handle, plus the persistent label "Monitoring Threshold (not a collision threshold)" вҖ” the caveat lives right next to the control that sets it, not buried elsewhere.
- Advanced params (window/step) collapsed by default to keep the primary view uncluttered.

### `DisclaimerBar`
- Fixed at the bottom, moderate-contrast (not screaming red, but genuinely readable вҖ” near-white text on the dark background, not tiny grey-on-black). Content: SGP4/TLE accuracy limitations + "screening/triage layer, not operational-grade" positioning, matching `PRD.md В§4`'s non-goals language.

### `PerceiveReasonExecuteStrip`
- Three segments, each dims/lights based on current pipeline stage (see `AppFlow.md В§2`). Small icon + label per stage. This is a deliberate, literal visualization of the judging rubric вҖ” not subtle, intentionally so.

## 7. Accessibility & Demo-Readability

- Never encode risk level by color alone вҖ” always pair with text label (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH) and, where practical, an icon (e.g. dot count or triangle fill level).
- Maintain WCAG-reasonable contrast between text and the dark background (near-white text `#E8ECF1` on `#05070D`/`#0B0F1A` comfortably clears this).
- Test the full flow at actual projector/screen-share resolution before the final recording вҖ” text and controls that look fine on a dev laptop can become illegible when screen-shared at a lower effective resolution.
- Ensure the "which satellites are at risk" answer is visible within the first 10 seconds of the Results state loading, with zero interaction required вҖ” this is the NFR from `PRD.md В§9` translated directly into a design constraint: the highest-risk `AlertCard` should be visually first (top of list, boldest badge) with no scrolling needed.

---

## 8. What NOT to Do (explicit anti-patterns, given the source material's warning)

- Do not ship a plain data table as the primary view with the 3D scene as an afterthought вҖ” the mentor guidance in the source material is explicit that this specific failure mode is what loses.
- Do not add so much motion/glow that the flagged conjunction stops being the single most eye-catching thing on screen.
- Do not hide the disclaimer in a tooltip or a collapsed "learn more" вҖ” visibility of the caveat is a stated credibility strategy, not boilerplate.
- Do not use CesiumJS's default photorealistic Earth imagery reflexively "because it looks cool" вҖ” it pulls in an external token dependency (see `TechSpec.md В§2.2`) that isn't needed for a stylized, deliberately mission-control aesthetic that we control fully.
